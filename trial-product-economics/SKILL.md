# Trial Product Economics & Pricing Psychology

Guides the design, pricing, and optimisation of trial products (sample packs, starter kits, discovery sets) for subscription products. The trial product is the primary conversion engine — it must be **self-supporting** (break-even or better) while maximising full-subscription conversion.

**When invoking**: On first use, open with 1–2 sentences on what this skill covers, then provide the main output. On subsequent use, go directly to the main output.

## Three Pricing Scenarios

### Scenario A: Free + Shipping

| Element | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Customer pays | £3.99–£4.99 (shipping only) |
| Brand receives | £3.99–£4.99 |
| Estimated cost to fulfil | £2.50–£3.50 |
| Gross margin per pack | £0.50–£2.49 |
| Required conversion to break even on CAC | 8–12% |

**Pros:** Lowest barrier to entry. Highest volume. Strong for cold traffic (Meta, TikTok).
**Cons:** Lower perceived value. Higher tyre-kicker rate. Need tight fraud/shipping-cost control.
**Best for:** Cold audience acquisition, top-of-funnel ads, viral loops.

### Scenario B: Low-Cost (£7.99–£9.99)

| Element | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Customer pays | £7.99–£9.99 |
| Brand receives | £7.99–£9.99 |
| Estimated cost to fulfil | £3.00–£4.00 |
| Gross margin per pack | £3.99–£6.99 |
| Required conversion to break even on CAC | 3–6% |

**Pros:** Filters for higher intent. Positive gross margin on the pack itself. Funds some ad spend.
**Cons:** 30–40% lower volume than free+shipping. Need stronger landing page copy.
**Best for:** Warm traffic (email list, retargeting, influencer referrals). Primary recommended scenario for Phase 0.

### Scenario C: Break-Even / Profit (£14.99–£19.99)

| Element | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Customer pays | £14.99–£19.99 |
| Brand receives | £14.99–£19.99 |
| Estimated cost to fulfil | £4.00–£5.50 |
| Gross margin per pack | £9.49–£15.99 |
| Required conversion to break even on CAC | Already profitable on pack alone |

**Pros:** Self-funding. Every pack sold generates cash. No CAC dependency.
**Cons:** 50–60% lower volume. Requires very strong value communication and social proof.
**Best for:** Established brand with reviews, email list, or organic community. NOT recommended for Phase 0.

## Break-Even Calculator

Use this formula to determine the required conversion rate for any trial product scenario:

```
Break-even conversion % = (Total Cost per Trial Pack) ÷ (Conversion Value × Gross Margin %)
```

Where:
- **Total Cost per Trial Pack** = Product COGS + Packaging + Postage + Payment fees + Ad cost (if applicable)
- **Conversion Value** = First-month subscription revenue + Immediate upsell revenue
- **Gross Margin %** = Your blended subscription gross margin (typically 60–70% for supplements)

## Pricing Psychology Frameworks

### 1. Anchoring

**Tactic:** Show the full subscription price prominently before revealing the trial pack price.

**Execution:**
- Landing page: "Experience £[XX] worth of daily nutrition for just £8.99"
- Ad creative: "£[XX]/month supplement routine — try it first for £8.99"
- Checkout page: Show strikethrough of full product value (£15–£20) next to trial price

**Why it works:** The brain judges value relative to the first number seen. £8.99 feels trivial next to £[XX].

### 2. Decoy Effect

**Tactic:** Offer three trial product options where the middle one is designed to push customers to the target option.

**Example structure:**

| Option | Contents | Price | Purpose |
|--------|----------|-------|---------|
| Mini | 3-day trial (base product only) | £4.99 | Low-commitment entry |
| **Standard** | **7-day trial (base bundle)** | **£8.99** | **Target conversion** |
| Premium | 14-day trial (base bundle + 1 add-on) | £18.99 | Makes Standard look like a bargain |

**Why it works:** The Premium option at £18.99 makes the Standard at £8.99 feel like a smart, economical choice. The Mini captures price-sensitive shoppers who might not convert otherwise.

### 3. Bundle Perception

**Tactic:** Frame the trial pack as a curated bundle, not individual products.

**Execution:**
- Name it: "The Daily Starter Kit" or "[Your Brand] Essentials Trial"
- Never list "Product A sachet: £2 + Product B sachet: £2" — this invites price breakdown
- Instead: "Complete 7-day nutrition system: £8.99"
- Include a printed ritual card ("Your Morning Routine") to reinforce the bundle as a system

**Why it works:** Bundles resist price comparison. The value is in the system, not the sum of parts.

### 4. Scarcity & Urgency

**Tactic:** Limit availability or time-frame to increase perceived value and action rate.

**Execution:**
- "Only 200 trial packs available this month" (true in Phase 0 with limited production budget)
- Countdown timer on landing page: "Offer ends Sunday at midnight"
- Post-purchase email: "Your 7-day trial starts now — 20% off your first subscription if you subscribe within 7 days"
- Limited flavours or "founding member" batch numbering

**Why it works:** Loss aversion is stronger than gain attraction. "I might miss out" drives action.

### 5. Risk Reversal

**Tactic:** Remove the perceived risk of trying.

**Execution:**
- "Not for you? No subscription, no commitment, no hard feelings."
- "Love it or it's free" guarantee on the trial pack (refund if they don't feel a difference)
- Clear cancellation messaging: "Subscribe any time. Cancel any time. No phone call required."

### CAC by Scenario

| Scenario | Trial Pack Price | Pack Cost | Pack Margin | Ad/Organic CAC | Total CAC | LTV:CAC (6mo) | LTV:CAC (12mo) |
|----------|------------------|-----------|-------------|----------------|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| A: Free + shipping | £4.99 | £3.50 | -£1.49 | £8.00 | £9.49 | 17.9× | 35.7× |

## Packaging & Presentation: Conversion Boosters

The unboxing experience directly impacts trial-to-subscription conversion. For a powder sachet format, presentation signals premium quality.

### Physical Packaging Checklist

| Element | Recommendation | Cost Estimate |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| **Outer mailer** | Kraft paper mailer or matte black pouch with [Your Brand] logo | £0.40–£0.70 |
| **Inner organisation** | Branded card sleeve holding 7 sachets; or small rigid box | £0.30–£0.60 |
| **Sachet design** | Matte finish, clear product name, day-of-week numbering (Mon–Sun) | Included in COGS |
| **Ritual card** | A6 card: "Your 7-Day Ritual" with mixing instructions and benefits timeline | £0.10–£0.15 |
| **QR code insert** | Links to: (1) Quiz for personalised add-ons, (2) Subscription checkout, (3) WhatsApp/SMS support | £0.05 |
| **Sticker seal** | Branded sticker sealing the mailer | £0.05–£0.10 |
| **Handwritten note** | In Phase 0, include a handwritten "Enjoy your ritual — [Founder Name]" card | £0.00 (your time) |

### Content of the Ritual Card

1. **Day 1–3:** "Your body is adjusting. Drink with breakfast. Notice your energy."
2. **Day 4–5:** "Gut microbes are colonising. Bloating may reduce."
3. **Day 6–7:** "You should feel the difference. Ready to make it daily?"
4. **QR code:** "Take the 60-second quiz to personalise your subscription"
5. **Offer:** "Subscribe within 7 days: 20% off first 3 months ([£XX.XX]/month)"

### Packaging Psychology Principles

- **Weight = quality:** A slightly heavier mailer (cardboard vs. plastic) signals premium
- **Texture matters:** Matte finishes feel more expensive than glossy
- **Colour consistency:** Use [Your Brand] brand colours throughout (refer to brand guidelines)
- **Day-of-week numbering:** Creates a ritual habit loop; missing "Wednesday" feels wrong
- **Personal touch in Phase 0:** Handwritten notes in first 100 packs create evangelists

## Tracking & Measurement

### Metrics to Track Weekly

| Metric | How to Track | Target |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Trial packs sold | E-commerce orders tagged "trial" | Baseline |
| Trial-to-subscription conversion | Email flow attribution + order tag | ≥15% |
| Days to conversion | Time from trial order to subscription order | ≤14 days |
| Trial pack gross margin | (Revenue − COGS − shipping) / Revenue | ≥30% |
| Blended CAC | Total marketing spend / New subscriptions | ≤£15 |
| LTV:CAC ratio | LTV / CAC | ≥3× |
| Subscription activation rate | % who complete quiz and first payment | ≥80% |
| 3-month retention | % still subscribed after 90 days | ≥60% |

### Tracking Setup (Phase 0 Tools)

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **E-commerce platform** | Tag all trial orders with "trial-pack-[variant]". Tag subscriptions with "sub-from-trial" when converted. |

## A/B Testing Plan

### Test Priority Matrix

| Priority | Test | Hypothesis | Success Metric |
|----------|------|-----------|----------------|
| **P1** | Price: £8.99 vs £12.99 | Higher price signals quality and increases conversion | Trial-to-sub rate |
| **P1** | Free + shipping (£4.99) vs Low-cost (£8.99) | Free generates volume; paid filters intent | Blended profit per 100 visitors |
| **P2** | 7-day vs 14-day trial | Longer trial = stronger habit = higher conversion | Trial-to-sub rate |
| **P2** | With add-on sample vs without | Tasting an add-on increases add-on subscription rate | Add-on attach rate |
| **P3** | Ritual card vs no ritual card | Ritual card increases perceived value and habit formation | Trial-to-sub rate |
| **P3** | Handwritten note vs printed | Personal touch increases conversion in Phase 0 | Trial-to-sub rate |
| **P4** | Urgency: countdown timer vs none | Scarcity increases checkout completion | Checkout conversion |
| **P4** | Guarantee: "Love it or free" vs none | Risk reversal removes hesitation | Trial-to-sub rate |

### Test Protocol

1. **Run one test at a time** on the trial product landing page
2. **Minimum sample size:** 100 conversions per variant (or 2 weeks, whichever is longer)
3. **Statistical significance:** 95% confidence before calling a winner
4. **Segment by traffic source:** Cold traffic behaves differently than warm
5. **Document in CRM:** Test name, hypothesis, variant A, variant B, result, date

### Phase 0 Testing Reality

With limited volume, statistical significance is hard to achieve. **Alternative approach:**
- Run sequential tests (Week 1: Price A, Week 2: Price B)
- Measure directional signals, not statistical certainty
- Trust qualitative feedback (replies to emails, support messages)
- When you hit 50+ trial conversions per variant, review for patterns

## Decision Framework: Which Scenario for [Your Brand]?

### Phase 0 (Launch, Months 1–3)

**Recommendation: Scenario B — Low-Cost (£8.99)**

| Factor | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Traffic source | Warm (network, email list, organic social, influencer) |
| Cash position | Self-supporting; every pack generates pack margin |
| Conversion target | 20% achievable with warm traffic and concierge follow-up |
| Risk | Low; positive margin on pack even with 0% conversion |
| Volume | Sufficient for first-month customer target |

### Phase 1 (Ads, Months 3–6)

**Recommendation: Test Scenario A — Free + Shipping (£4.99) for cold traffic**

| Factor | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Traffic source | Cold Meta/TikTok ads |
| Volume need | Higher; ads need scale |
| Conversion target | 15% with strong creative and retargeting |
| Risk | Higher; pack is near break-even. Requires tight cost control. |

### Phase 2 (Scale, Months 6+)

**Recommendation: Hybrid — Scenario A for cold, Scenario B for warm**

- Cold traffic → Free + shipping landing page
- Email list / retargeting → Low-cost landing page
- Organic / referral → Low-cost or break-even
