# Novel Foods Reference for [Your Brand] Ingredients

> Originally developed for a supplement subscription business, now genericized for universal use.

## What Is a Novel Food?

A novel food is any food or food ingredient that was **not consumed to a significant degree in the EU before 15 May 1997**. Under Regulation 2015/2283, novel foods require **pre-market authorisation** before they can be placed on the EU or UK market.

**Critical rule:** If an ingredient is novel and not authorised, **you cannot use it**. No exceptions. Not even at low doses.

### Key Resources

- **EU Novel Food Catalogue:** https://food.ec.europa.eu/safety/novel-food/catalogue_en
- **GB (UK) Novel Food Register:** https://www.food.gov.uk/business-guidance/authorised-novel-foods
- **FSA Novel Food Guidance:** https://www.food.gov.uk/business-guidance/novel-foods

## How to Verify Novel Food Status

### Step 1: Check the EU Novel Food Catalogue
1. Go to: https://food.ec.europa.eu/safety/novel-food/catalogue_en
2. Search for the ingredient by name (try common names, scientific names, Latin names)
3. Read the result carefully:
   - **"Not novel"** = Safe to use
   - **"Novel"** = Cannot use unless you find a specific authorisation
   - **"Novel - authorisation required"** = Check the Union List
   - **"Not in catalogue / no data"** = Presumed novel; requires verification

### Step 2: Check the GB Novel Foods Register
1. Go to: https://www.food.gov.uk/business-guidance/authorised-novel-foods
2. Search for the ingredient
3. Post-Brexit, the UK maintains its own register

### Step 3: Check the EU Union List of Novel Foods
1. Go to: https://food.ec.europa.eu/safety/novel-food/union-list-novel-foods_en
2. If the ingredient IS novel, check if it has an approved entry on the Union List
3. Each entry specifies:
   - The specific form authorised
   - Maximum usage levels
   - Any restrictions

### Step 4: Supplier Documentation
Request from your supplier:
- [ ] Novel food status confirmation letter
- [ ] Evidence of significant consumption in EU before 1997 (if claiming not novel)
- [ ] Copy of novel food authorisation (if applicable)
- [ ] Specification sheet showing the exact form/manufacturing process

### Step 5: When in Doubt
If the ingredient's status is unclear:
1. Do NOT use it until clarified
2. Contact the FSA (UK) or EFSA (EU) for informal guidance
3. Consult a food regulatory specialist
4. Consider an alternative ingredient with clear status

## Novel Food Application Process (If You Need One)

**Timeline:** 12-24 months minimum
**Cost:** €50,000–€200,000+ (safety studies, dossier preparation)
**Outcome:** Not guaranteed

**Startup context:** As a new business with a limited first production budget, **do NOT attempt novel food applications.** Only use ingredients that are:
1. Clearly not novel, OR
2. Already authorised on the Union List / GB Register
