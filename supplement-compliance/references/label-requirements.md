# Label Requirements for UK/EU Food Supplements

> Originally developed for a supplement subscription business, now genericized for universal use.

## Legal Basis

- **EU Regulation 1169/2011** — Food Information to Consumers (FIC)
- **Directive 2002/46/EC** — Food Supplements (as amended)
- **The Food Information Regulations 2014** (UK)
- **The Food Supplements (England) Regulations 2003** (and equivalents for Scotland, Wales, NI)

---

## Mandatory Label Elements

Every food supplement sold in the UK must display the following on the pack:

### 1. Product Identity

- **"Food Supplement"** — must be clearly stated
- **Product name** — descriptive and not misleading
- **Net quantity** — weight or number of portions (e.g., "30 x 5g sachets", "Net weight: 150g")

### 2. Ingredients List

- Listed in **descending order by weight** at the time of manufacture
- All ingredients must be named using:
  - **Legal names** where defined (e.g., "ascorbic acid" for Vitamin C)
  - **Common/usual names** where no legal name exists
  - **Category name + specific name** for additives (e.g., "colour: curcumin" or "colour: E100")
- **Allergens** must be highlighted (bold, contrasting colour, capitals, or underline)
  - The 14 EU allergens: cereals containing gluten, crustaceans, eggs, fish, peanuts, soybeans, milk, nuts, celery, mustard, sesame, sulphur dioxide, lupin, molluscs
- **Exemptions:** Individual sachets within a multipack may not need full ingredients list if the outer pack has it, but best practice includes it.

### 3. Nutritional Information

For food supplements, this means:

- **Recommended daily dose** — e.g., "Take 1 sachet (5g) daily"
- **Nutritional declaration per daily dose** — amounts of all vitamins, minerals, and other active substances
- **% NRV (Nutrient Reference Value)** for vitamins and minerals — calculated per daily dose
  - NRVs are defined in Regulation 1169/2011 Annex XIII
  - Common NRVs: Vitamin C = 80mg, Vitamin D = 5µg, Iron = 14mg, Zinc = 10mg, Magnesium = 375mg
- **Energy value** (kJ and kcal) — if the product provides significant energy (usually required)
- **Fat, carbohydrate, protein, salt** — if making a nutrition claim about these (usually not applicable to powder supplements)

### 4. Vitamins & Minerals Information

Per Directive 2002/46/EC:

- Only vitamins and minerals on the **Permitted Lists** may be used
- Only permitted **chemical forms** may be used (Annex II of 2002/46/EC)
- Amounts must be declared per **daily dose**, not per 100g
- %NRV must be shown

### 5. Health Claims

- Any health claims on the pack must be from the **EU Register**
- Must use **exact authorised wording**
- Must meet the **condition of use** (e.g., minimum dose)
- A **reference to the claim's ID** on the Register is recommended but not legally required

### 6. Name and Address of Food Business Operator (FBO)

- The **FBO** (the entity responsible for compliance) must be named
- Full **UK address** must be shown
- If the FBO is outside the UK, the **UK importer** must be named with their UK address
- For Northern Ireland, an EU address is still required under Windsor Framework
- **Email or web address** is recommended but not mandatory

### 7. Country of Origin

- Required if **omission would mislead** the consumer
- Required if the product's origin is a selling point (e.g., "Made in USA" must be stated)
- Good practice: always state if manufactured outside UK/EU

### 8. Best Before Date / Use By Date

- **"Best before: [date]"** — for supplements (they don't spoil quickly)
- **"Use by: [date]"** — only for highly perishable foods (rarely applicable to supplements)
- Must be shown in order: day, month, year (or month, year if shelf life >3 months)

### 9. Batch / Lot Number

- **Lot mark or batch code** — required for traceability
- Format: "Lot: ABC123" or "Batch: XYZ789"
- Must allow identification of the production batch

### 10. Storage Conditions

- Required if product needs specific storage
- Examples: "Store in a cool, dry place", "Keep out of direct sunlight", "Refrigerate after opening"
- For powder sachets: "Store in a cool, dry place below 25°C" is standard

### 11. Warnings / Advisory Statements

**Mandatory in some cases:**

- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding warning:** If product contains ingredients not recommended (e.g., high-dose vitamin A, certain herbs)
- **Children warning:** If not suitable for children
- **Medical conditions warning:** "If you are taking any medications or have any medical conditions, consult your doctor before use"
- **Do not exceed stated dose**
- **Food supplements should not be used as a substitute for a varied diet**
- **Keep out of reach of children**

**Caffeine warning (if >150mg per serving):**
> "High caffeine content. Not recommended for children or pregnant or breast-feeding women."

**Vitamin A warning (if >800µg retinol equivalents):**
> "Do not take if you are pregnant or planning to become pregnant, except on the advice of a doctor or antenatal clinic."

**Beta-carotene warning:**
> "Do not take if you are a smoker."

### 12. Directions for Use

- Clear instructions on how to consume
- E.g., "Mix one sachet with 200ml water and consume once daily"
- Timing: "Take in the morning with food" or "Take before bed" if relevant

### 13. Quantity of Certain Ingredients

If you make a claim about a specific ingredient (e.g., "Contains 5g of inulin"), the quantity of that ingredient per serving must be stated.

## What NOT to Include on the Label

| Prohibited Element | Why |
|-------------------|-----|
| **Medicinal claims** | Illegal for foods |
| **Unauthorised health claims** | Violates 1924/2006 |
| **"Doctor recommended"** (without evidence) | Misleading |
| **"100% safe" / "No side effects"** | Misleading; impossible to guarantee |
| **"Natural"** (without justification) | Vague and potentially misleading |
| **"Organic"** (without certification) | Misleading; must have Soil Association/EU organic certification |
| **"Chemical-free"** | All substances are chemicals; misleading |
| **"Proven" / "Clinically proven"** (without specific trial for this exact product) | Misleading |
| **"FDA approved"** | FDA does not approve supplements; false |
| **"MHRA approved"** | MHRA does not approve food supplements; false |

## Allergen Labelling — The 14 Allergens

All of these must be highlighted in the ingredients list:

1. Cereals containing gluten (wheat, rye, barley, oats, spelt, kamut)
2. Crustaceans
3. Eggs
4. Fish
5. Peanuts
6. Soybeans
7. Milk (including lactose)
8. Nuts (almonds, hazelnuts, walnuts, cashews, pecans, Brazil nuts, pistachios, macadamia, Queensland nuts)
9. Celery
10. Mustard
11. Sesame
12. Sulphur dioxide and sulphites (at concentrations >10mg/kg or 10mg/L)
13. Lupin
14. Molluscs

**May contain warnings:**
- "May contain traces of [allergen]" — only use if there is a genuine risk of cross-contamination
- Do not use as a blanket disclaimer
- Must be based on a risk assessment of your manufacturing facility

## Font Size & Legibility Requirements

- **x-height** of characters must be ≥1.2mm (for packs >80cm² surface area)
- For very small packs (<80cm²), x-height can be ≥0.9mm
- Must be **easily visible, clearly legible, and indelible**
- Must not be **hidden, obscured, or interrupted**
- **Contrast** must be sufficient against background

## Claims on Front of Pack

Be careful with front-of-pack messaging:

| What you CAN put on front | What you CANNOT put on front |
|--------------------------|------------------------------|
| "Food Supplement" | "Boosts immunity" |
| Product name (descriptive) | "Natural energy booster" |
| "With Vitamin C & Zinc" | "Prevents colds" |
| "30 sachets" | "Cures fatigue" |
| Visual of key ingredients | Medical imagery (pills, syringes, etc.) |
| "Daily wellness blend" | "Doctor formulated" (unless true and substantiated) |

## E-Numbers and Additives

If using additives, you must label them using either:
- The **E-number** (e.g., "E300")
- The **chemical name** (e.g., "Ascorbic acid")
- A **category + E-number** (e.g., "Antioxidant: E300")

**Common additives in powder supplements:**

| Function | Common Additives | E-Numbers |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Sweetener | Steviol glycosides, Sucralose | E960, E955 |
| Anti-caking | Silicon dioxide, Magnesium stearate | E551, E470b |
| Acid | Citric acid, Malic acid | E330, E296 |
| Flavouring | Natural flavouring | — |
| Colour | Beetroot red, Turmeric | E162, E100 |
| Bulking agent | Maltodextrin, Inulin | — |
| Emulsifier | Lecithin | E322 |

**Note:** "Natural flavouring" is a category name and does not need further specification unless a specific allergen is present (e.g., "natural flavouring (contains milk)").

## Country-Specific Variations

### UK (England, Wales, Scotland)
- English language mandatory
- FSA notification recommended (notified to FSA before marketing)
- FBO address in UK

### Northern Ireland
- Still follows EU food law under Windsor Framework
- EU address of FBO or EU importer required
- May need EU notification

### Republic of Ireland (if selling there)
- Irish/English bilingual or English alone acceptable
- Follows EU law directly
- EU address required

### EU Member States (if expanding)
- Local language required
- EU address required
- Some member states have additional restrictions (e.g., Italy on "probiotic", some on botanicals)
