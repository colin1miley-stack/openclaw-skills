# Health Claims Guide for [Your Brand] Products

> Originally developed for a supplement subscription business, now genericized for universal use.

## The Rule

Only health claims **on the EU Register of Nutrition and Health Claims** (Regulation 1924/2006) may be used. Claims must be used in the **exact authorised wording** (or an acceptable translation). You may not paraphrase, embellish, or combine claims to imply something stronger.

**EU Register:** https://ec.europa.eu/food/safety/labelling_nutrition/claims/register/public/

---

## How to Read a Health Claim

Each claim on the Register specifies:
- The **exact wording** (or approved variations)
- The **nutrient/substance** it applies to
- The **condition of use** (e.g., minimum daily amount required)
- A **claim ID** for reference

If your product does not meet the condition of use (e.g., not enough of the nutrient), you **cannot use the claim**, even if the ingredient is present.

## Permitted vs Prohibited — By Product Category

### 1. Core Daily (Multivitamin/Multimineral)

**What you CAN say (examples from EU Register):**

| Approved Claim | Exact Wording (or acceptable variant) | Condition |
|---------------|--------------------------------------|-----------|
| Vitamin C | "Vitamin C contributes to the normal function of the immune system" | Any amount contributing to ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin D | "Vitamin D contributes to the maintenance of normal bones" | ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin B12 | "Vitamin B12 contributes to the reduction of tiredness and fatigue" | ≥15% NRV |
| Iron | "Iron contributes to normal cognitive function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Zinc | "Zinc contributes to normal fertility and reproduction" | ≥15% NRV |
| Magnesium | "Magnesium contributes to normal muscle function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Folic acid | "Folic acid contributes to normal maternal tissue growth during pregnancy" | ≥200 µg/day |
| Biotin | "Biotin contributes to the maintenance of normal hair" | ≥15% NRV |
| Niacin | "Niacin contributes to normal psychological function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Pantothenic acid | "Pantothenic acid contributes to normal mental performance" | ≥15% NRV |

**What you CANNOT say:**

| Prohibited Claim | Why It's Illegal | Safer Alternative |
|-----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| "Boosts your immune system" | "Boosts" implies enhancement beyond normal function; not on Register | Use the exact approved claim: "Vitamin C contributes to the normal function of the immune system" |
| "Strengthens bones" | Too strong; "maintenance of normal bones" is the approved wording | Use exact wording from Register |
| "Prevents osteoporosis" | Medicinal claim (prevents disease) | Remove entirely |
| "Gives you more energy" | Implies physiological enhancement beyond normal; not on Register | Use: "[B-vitamin] contributes to normal energy-yielding metabolism" |
| "Supercharges your body" | No basis in the Register; puffery not allowed | Remove entirely |
| "100% daily protection" | Implies complete coverage; misleading | Remove entirely |

### 2. Gut Microbe (Probiotic/Prebiotic)

**Critical note:** The term "probiotic" itself is **not a health claim** but its use is restricted in some EU member states (notably Italy prohibits the word on food labels). In the UK, "probiotic" can be used but **only if not linked to a specific health claim** unless that claim is on the Register.

**What you CAN say:**

| Approved Claim | Exact Wording | Condition |
|---------------|--------------|-----------|
| Dietary fibre / inulin | "Inulin contributes to normal bowel function" | ≥5g inulin/day |
| Live cultures (general) | "Contains live cultures" | Must be true; not a health claim |
| Calcium | "Calcium contributes to the normal function of digestive enzymes" | ≥15% NRV |
| Specific authorised probiotic strains | Some strains have authorised claims (e.g., Lactobacillus-specific claims) | Must use the exact authorised strain |

**What you CANNOT say:**

| Prohibited Claim | Why It's Illegal | Safer Alternative |
|-----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| "Balances your gut microbiome" | "Balances" is not an approved claim; implies therapeutic effect | Use: "Contains live cultures" or "Source of dietary fibre" |
| "Restores healthy gut bacteria" | "Restores" implies correction of a dysfunction → medicinal | Use: "Contains [specific strain]" with count |
| "Heals your gut" | Medicinal claim (healing) | Remove entirely |
| "Probiotic — improves digestion" | "Improves digestion" is not on the Register | Use: "Inulin contributes to normal bowel function" (if ≥5g) |
| "Prevents IBS / diarrhoea / bloating" | Prevents disease → medicinal | Remove entirely |
| "Detoxifies your gut" | "Detox" is banned across EU/UK for foods | Remove entirely |

**Strain-specific claims:** Some specific probiotic strains (e.g., certain Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium strains) have approved health claims on the Register. These are strain-specific and often company-specific. If using a branded strain (e.g., Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG), check if the manufacturer holds an authorised claim for that exact strain.

### 3. Sleep

**CRITICAL: Melatonin is NOT permitted in UK food supplements.**

In the UK, melatonin is classified as a **prescription-only medicine**. It cannot be sold as a food supplement. Some EU countries allow melatonin in supplements at low doses (e.g., 1mg in some member states), but the UK does not.

**What you CAN say (with magnesium, L-theanine, glycine, etc.):**

| Approved Claim | Exact Wording | Condition |
|---------------|--------------|-----------|
| Magnesium | "Magnesium contributes to normal functioning of the nervous system" | ≥15% NRV |
| Magnesium | "Magnesium contributes to normal psychological function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Biotin / B vitamins | "[B-vitamin] contributes to the normal functioning of the nervous system" | ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin B6 | "Vitamin B6 contributes to the regulation of hormonal activity" | ≥15% NRV |

**Note:** "Regulation of hormonal activity" (for B6) refers broadly to hormonal systems, not specifically to sleep hormones. Do not link this to melatonin or sleep directly.

**What you CANNOT say:**

| Prohibited Claim | Why It's Illegal | Safer Alternative |
|-----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| "Helps you fall asleep faster" | Improves sleep onset; not an approved claim for any food substance | Use: "Magnesium contributes to normal functioning of the nervous system" |
| "Promotes deep sleep" | Not on Register; implies modification of sleep architecture | Remove or use nervous system claim |
| "Improves sleep quality" | Not an approved claim | Remove entirely |
| "Natural sleeping aid" | "Aid" implies treatment of a condition → medicinal | Remove entirely |
| "Melatonin formula" | Melatonin is a medicine in the UK; illegal to sell as food | Reformulate without melatonin |
| "Contains melatonin" | Same as above; illegal | Do not use melatonin |
| "Calms your mind for better sleep" | Links a food claim to a specific sleep outcome not on Register | Use: "Magnesium contributes to normal psychological function" |

**Permitted non-claim language (careful):**
You may describe the **formulation** without making health claims: "A soothing evening blend with magnesium, glycine, and chamomile." This is product description, not a claim, but be careful not to imply outcomes.

### 4. Stress

**Key ingredients often used:** Ashwagandha, Rhodiola, Magnesium, B-vitamins, L-theanine.

**What you CAN say:**

| Approved Claim | Exact Wording | Condition |
|---------------|--------------|-----------|
| Magnesium | "Magnesium contributes to normal psychological function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin B5 (pantothenic acid) | "Pantothenic acid contributes to normal mental performance" | ≥15% NRV |
| Niacin | "Niacin contributes to normal psychological function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin B6 | "Vitamin B6 contributes to the regulation of hormonal activity" | ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin B12 / Folate | "[B12/Folate] contributes to the reduction of tiredness and fatigue" | ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin C | "Vitamin C contributes to normal psychological function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Iodine | "Iodine contributes to normal cognitive function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Zinc | "Zinc contributes to normal cognitive function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Iron | "Iron contributes to normal cognitive function" | ≥15% NRV |

**Ashwagandha specific:**
Ashwagandha root extract (specifically KSM-66 or Sensoril branded extracts) may have authorised claims in some contexts, but the botanical itself is a **novel food**. Only use forms that have been authorised. The claim landscape for ashwagandha is limited — do not make stress/anxiety claims unless the specific extract has an approved claim on the Register.

**What you CANNOT say:**

| Prohibited Claim | Why It's Illegal | Safer Alternative |
|-----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| "Reduces stress and anxiety" | "Reduces anxiety" implies treatment of a medical condition → medicinal | Use approved B-vitamin claims about psychological function |
| "Calms your nerves" | "Calms" implies pharmacological action → medicinal | Use exact Register wording |
| "Lowers cortisol" | "Lowers" implies modification of a hormone → medicinal/borderline | Remove entirely |
| "Adaptogen that helps your body adapt to stress" | "Adaptogen" is not a recognised food term; implies pharmacological mechanism | Remove; describe ingredients without mechanism |
| "Anti-stress formula" | "Anti-stress" implies treatment/prevention → medicinal | Use: "With magnesium and B-vitamins" |
| "Balances your mood" | Not on Register; implies correction of mood disorder → medicinal | Use approved cognitive/psychological claims |
| "Reduces panic attacks" | Treats a medical condition → medicinal | Remove entirely |

|---------------|--------------|-----------|

| Protein | "Protein contributes to the maintenance of normal bones" | Any amount contributing to ≥12g protein/day |
| Magnesium | "Magnesium contributes to normal muscle function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Magnesium | "Magnesium contributes to electrolyte balance" | ≥15% NRV |
| Potassium | "Potassium contributes to normal muscle function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin C | "Vitamin C contributes to normal collagen formation for the normal function of cartilage" | ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin D | "Vitamin D contributes to the maintenance of normal muscle function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin D | "Vitamin D contributes to the maintenance of normal bones" | ≥15% NRV |
| Calcium | "Calcium contributes to normal muscle function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Manganese | "Manganese contributes to the normal formation of connective tissue" | ≥15% NRV |
| Copper | "Copper contributes to the maintenance of normal connective tissues" | ≥15% NRV |

**What you CANNOT say:**

| Prohibited Claim | Why It's Illegal | Safer Alternative |
|-----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| "Reduces inflammation" | "Reduces inflammation" implies treatment of a pathological state → medicinal | Use: "Vitamin C contributes to normal collagen formation" |
| "Speeds up recovery" | Not on Register; implies enhanced recovery beyond normal | Use: "Protein contributes to the growth and maintenance of muscle mass" |

**Turmeric/Curcumin note:** Curcuminoids are not on the EU Register with any health claims. You can list turmeric as an ingredient but **cannot make any health claims about it**. It is also a novel food in some contexts — verify status.

**CoQ10 note:** Coenzyme Q10 is a **novel food** in the EU. Only specific forms are authorised. Check the EU Novel Food Catalogue. No health claims for CoQ10 are on the EU Register, so you can list it as an ingredient but cannot make any claims about it.

**Caffeine note:** If including caffeine, be aware that:
- The EU requires warnings for products >150mg caffeine per serving: "High caffeine content. Not recommended for children or pregnant or breast-feeding women."
- The UK recommends similar warnings.
- Do not exceed ~200mg per day in supplements (EFSA safe limit for adults is 400mg/day from all sources).

**Ginseng note:** Panax ginseng is not a novel food (traditional use). However, **no health claims for ginseng** are on the EU Register. You can list it as an ingredient but cannot make claims about it.

### 7. Hormone Balance

**Key ingredients often used:** Maca, vitex (chasteberry), B-vitamins, magnesium, zinc, vitamin D.

**CRITICAL WARNING:** This is a **high-risk category** for borderline medicinal classification. Any claim about "balancing hormones", "regulating hormones" (except the specific B6 claim), or modifying endocrine function risks MHRA reclassification.

**What you CAN say:**

| Approved Claim | Exact Wording | Condition |
|---------------|--------------|-----------|
| Vitamin B6 | "Vitamin B6 contributes to the regulation of hormonal activity" | ≥15% NRV |
| Zinc | "Zinc contributes to normal fertility and reproduction" | ≥15% NRV |
| Zinc | "Zinc contributes to the maintenance of normal testosterone levels in the blood" | ≥15% NRV |
| Selenium | "Selenium contributes to normal spermatogenesis" | ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin D | "Vitamin D has a role in the process of cell division" | ≥15% NRV |
| Folate | "Folate contributes to normal maternal tissue growth during pregnancy" | ≥200 µg/day |
| Iodine | "Iodine contributes to the normal production of thyroid hormones" | ≥15% NRV |
| Iodine | "Iodine contributes to normal thyroid function" | ≥15% NRV |

**What you CANNOT say:**

| Prohibited Claim | Why It's Illegal | Safer Alternative |
|-----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| "Balances your hormones" | "Balances hormones" implies correction of hormonal dysfunction → medicinal | Remove entirely; this is the riskiest phrase |
| "Regulates estrogen/progesterone" | Implies modification of specific hormones → medicinal | Remove entirely |
| "Supports thyroid function" (if implying treatment) | "Supports" alone may be okay if vague, but "supports thyroid function" borders on medicinal if the thyroid is diseased | Use exact Register wording: "Iodine contributes to normal thyroid function" |
| "Helps with PMS/menopause symptoms" | Treats symptoms of a condition → medicinal | Remove entirely |
| "Natural HRT alternative" | Direct comparison to hormone replacement therapy → medicinal | Remove entirely |
| "Optimises your endocrine system" | "Optimises" implies enhancement beyond normal → medicinal | Remove entirely |
| "DHEA for hormonal support" | DHEA is a prescription medicine in the UK. **Never use.** | Do not use DHEA. |

**Maca note:** Maca root is a **novel food** but has been authorised in the EU/UK for use in food supplements. You can use it but **cannot make any health claims about it** (no maca claims on the Register).

**Vitex (Chasteberry) note:** Vitex agnus-castus is **not a novel food** but is used in traditional herbal medicines in the EU. Using it in a food supplement with any implied medicinal claim risks borderline classification. No approved food health claims exist for vitex. Use with extreme caution.

### 8. Men's Vitality / Testosterone Support

**This is the highest-risk category for compliance.** Any implication that the product affects testosterone levels beyond the single approved zinc claim risks medicinal classification.

**What you CAN say:**

| Approved Claim | Exact Wording | Condition |
|---------------|--------------|-----------|
| Zinc | "Zinc contributes to the maintenance of normal testosterone levels in the blood" | ≥15% NRV |
| Zinc | "Zinc contributes to normal fertility and reproduction" | ≥15% NRV |
| Selenium | "Selenium contributes to normal spermatogenesis" | ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin D | "Vitamin D contributes to the maintenance of normal muscle function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin D | "Vitamin D contributes to the maintenance of normal bones" | ≥15% NRV |
| Magnesium | "Magnesium contributes to normal muscle function" | ≥15% NRV |
| Vitamin B6 | "Vitamin B6 contributes to the regulation of hormonal activity" | ≥15% NRV |

**What you CANNOT say (this list is exhaustive for this category):**

| Prohibited Claim | Why It's Illegal | Safer Alternative |
|-----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| "Boosts testosterone" | "Boosts" implies enhancement beyond maintenance of normal levels → medicinal | Use ONLY: "Zinc contributes to the maintenance of normal testosterone levels in the blood" |
| "Increases testosterone" | Same as above; "increases" implies raising levels | Use the exact zinc claim only |
| "Supports healthy testosterone" | Vague but risks implying correction of low testosterone | Use the exact zinc claim only |
| "Restores testosterone levels" | "Restores" implies correction of deficiency → medicinal | Remove entirely |
| "Natural testosterone booster" | "Booster" implies enhancement → medicinal; also many "T-boosters" have been banned | Remove entirely |
| "Enhances male vitality" | "Enhances" implies beyond normal; "vitality" is vague puffery | Use approved mineral claims |
| "Improves libido / sex drive" | Not on Register; implies treatment of sexual dysfunction → medicinal | Remove entirely |
| "Builds muscle by increasing testosterone" | Links testosterone to muscle building; makes an unauthorised implied claim | Use: "Protein contributes to the growth and maintenance of muscle mass" |
| "D-Aspartic acid (DAA) increases testosterone" | DAA is a novel food (amino acid); no approved claims; "increases testosterone" is medicinal | Do not use DAA in UK food supplements without novel food approval. Even then, no testosterone claims allowed. |
| "Fenugreek boosts free testosterone" | Fenugreek is not a novel food but has no approved health claims on the Register; "boosts testosterone" is medicinal | List as ingredient only; make no claims |
| "Tongkat ali / Longjack for testosterone" | Tongkat ali (Eurycoma longifolia) is a **novel food**. Do not use without authorisation. | Do not use. |
| "Tribulus terrestris for testosterone" | Tribulus is a **novel food** in the EU. Do not use without authorisation. | Do not use. |

**D-Aspartic Acid (DAA) specific:**
DAA is an amino acid. Amino acids in food supplements are **highly restricted** in the EU/UK. Under EC 1925/2006, only amino acids on the Permitted List may be added to foods, and the list is very short. DAA is **not on the Permitted List** and is considered a novel food. **Do not use DAA in UK food supplements.**

**Fenugreek specific:** Fenugreek is a traditional food in some EU countries, so it is not a novel food. However, **no health claims for fenugreek** are on the EU Register. You can include it as an ingredient but cannot make any claims about it.

## General Prohibited Claims (All Products)

These claims are never permitted for food supplements in the EU/UK:

| Prohibited Term/Phrase | Reason |
|----------------------|--------|
| "Detox" / "Detoxifies" | Banned; no scientific basis accepted for foods |
| "Cleanse" (implying body cleansing) | Implies removal of toxins; not supported |
| "Superfood" | Puffery; implies extraordinary properties |
| "Miracle" / "Magical" / "Secret" | Exaggerated and misleading |
| "Clinically proven" | Unless you have a specific clinical trial for the exact claim on the Register, this is misleading |
| "Doctor recommended" | Unless you have documented evidence of a specific doctor's recommendation for this exact product |
| "100% natural" | Meaningless without definition; can be challenged |
| "Chemical-free" | All substances are chemicals; misleading |
| "No side effects" | Implies the product is so safe it cannot cause harm; potentially misleading and medicinal-adjacent |
| "Safe for everyone" | Absolute claim; potentially misleading |
| "FDA approved" / "Approved by the FDA" | The FDA does not approve food supplements; this is false and misleading |
| "MHRA approved" | Same as above; MHRA does not approve food supplements |
| "Cures" / "Treats" / "Prevents" / "Heals" / "Restores" | Medicinal claims; illegal for foods |
| "Anti-" (anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, anti-ageing, etc.) | Implies prevention/treatment of disease or pathological condition |
| "Boosts immunity" (unless exact Register wording) | "Boosts" is not on the Register; "contributes to the normal function of the immune system" is the approved form |

## Social Media & Website Copy Guidance

The same health claim rules apply to **all marketing materials**, including:
- Product pages on your website
- Instagram/Facebook/TikTok posts and captions
- Email marketing
- Influencer briefs (you are responsible for claims made on your behalf)
- Customer reviews/testimonials that you feature (you become responsible for their claims)

**Best practice for social media:**
1. Create a **pre-approved claim bank** for each product
2. Brief any influencers or content creators with **only approved wording**
3. Monitor UGC (user-generated content) that you repost — you adopt liability for claims in reposted content
4. Use "#ad" and "#foodsupplement" hashtags as required by ASA/CAP rules

**CAP Code (UK advertising):** The Advertising Standards Authority enforces rules on advertising claims. CAP Code rule 15.1 specifically covers food products. Familiarise yourself with: https://www.asa.org.uk/codes-and-rulings/cap-codes.html

## Summary Checklist for Any Marketing Copy

Before publishing any product description, social post, or email:

- [ ] Every health claim is on the EU Register
- [ ] Every claim uses the exact approved wording
- [ ] The claim's condition of use is met by the product formulation
- [ ] No medicinal language (treat, cure, prevent, heal, restore)
- [ ] No "detox", "superfood", "boosts" (unless exact Register wording)
- [ ] No absolute claims ("100%", "everyone", "always")
- [ ] Influencers/content creators briefed with approved claims only
- [ ] Legal review for high-risk categories (testosterone, hormone balance, sleep)
