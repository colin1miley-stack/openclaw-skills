# Supplier Evaluation Rubric

> Originally developed for a supplement subscription business, now genericized for universal use.

Score every supplier 1–5 on each criterion. Multiply by weight, sum for a total out of 100. Any supplier scoring below 3 on a must-have criterion is automatically disqualified.

---

## Scoring Guide

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Exceeds expectations — best-in-class |
| 4 | Meets expectations — solid, no concerns |
| 3 | Acceptable — meets minimum but has gaps |
| 2 | Below standard — significant concerns |
| 1 | Unacceptable — do not proceed |

---

## Criterion Definitions

### 1. GMP Certification (Must-Have)
**Weight:** Disqualify if < 3

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | GMP + ISO 9001 + BRC/FSSC 22000; certificates verifiable online |
| 4 | GMP + one additional cert; certificate provided |
| 3 | GMP only; certificate number given and verifiable |
| 2 | Claims GMP but can't provide certificate or number |
| 1 | No GMP; "GMP-equivalent" only; refuses to discuss |

**Verification:** Ask for the certificate number and the certifying body. Look it up on the certifying body's website (e.g., SGS, BSI, NSF).

---

### 2. Powder Blending Capability (Must-Have)
**Weight:** Disqualify if < 3

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | In-house blending with validated recipes; R&D lab for NPD; multiple blend types (V-blender, ribbon blender, fluid bed) |
| 4 | In-house blending; can handle complex multi-ingredient powders; offers NPD support |
| 3 | In-house blending; basic capabilities; may outsource some ingredients |
| 2 | Outsources blending; limited control over process |
| 1 | No blending capability; only repacks pre-mixed powders |

**Questions to ask:** "Do you blend in-house?" "What's the largest number of actives you've blended in one product?" "Do you have a V-blender or ribbon blender?" "Can you handle hygroscopic powders?"

---

### 3. Sachet / Stick-Pack Filling (Must-Have)
**Weight:** Disqualify if < 3

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Multi-lane stick-pack line; 10+ years experience; can do 1g–30g sachets; offers single-dose and multi-dose |
| 4 | Stick-pack filling; standard sizes; good experience |
| 3 | Sachet filling (vertical form-fill-seal); not stick-pack specific |
| 2 | Can fill sachets but mainly does jars/tubs |
| 1 | No sachet capability; only bottles or tubs |

**Note:** Stick-packs (long, narrow sachets) look more premium than standard pillow-pouch sachets. Ask which they offer.

---

### 4. Allergen Handling (Must-Have)
**Weight:** Disqualify if < 3

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Dedicated allergen-free line; full HACCP plan; validated clean-down with swab testing; can provide allergen risk assessment |
| 4 | Separate production runs for allergen-free; validated clean-down; allergen policy documented |
| 3 | Same line but with clean-down; basic allergen management |
| 2 | Claims allergen management but no documentation; vague on process |
| 1 | No allergen management; cross-contamination risk |

**Brand-specific note:** Many supplement ingredients are allergen-free by nature, but if you add anything with soy, dairy, or gluten (e.g., some probiotics grown on dairy), this becomes critical.

---

### 5. Price per Unit (High Weight: ×3)

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | ≤ £3.00 at MOQ; strong value |
| 4 | £3.01–£3.50 |
| 3 | £3.51–£4.00 |
| 2 | £4.01–£5.00 |
| 1 | > £5.00 |

**Note:** This is all-in cost per sachet (raw materials + blending + filling + packaging). At [Base price]/month for ~30 sachets, your cost of goods needs to stay under a viable unit cost to maintain margin after 3PL, shipping, and marketing.

---

### 6. MOQ Flexibility (High Weight: ×3)

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | ≤ 250 units; happy to do trial batches; startup-friendly pricing |
| 4 | 250–500 units; some flexibility on first order |
| 3 | 500–1,000 units; standard MOQ but open to discussion |
| 2 | 1,000–2,500 units; rigid on MOQ |
| 1 | > 2,500 units; no flexibility |

---

### 7. Lead Time (Medium Weight: ×2)

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | ≤ 3 weeks |
| 4 | 4–5 weeks |
| 3 | 6–8 weeks |
| 2 | 9–12 weeks |
| 1 | > 12 weeks |

---

### 8. Minimum Run Size Without Penalty (Medium Weight: ×2)

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | No minimum; pay per unit only |
| 4 | ≤ 250 units with small setup fee |
| 3 | 250–500 units |
| 2 | 500–1,000 units with large setup fee |
| 1 | > 1,000 units; no small-batch option |

---

### 9. Labelling / Regulatory Support (Medium Weight: ×2)

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Full regulatory support; provides compliant label artwork; understands UK/EU supplement regulations (SI 2003/1247, EU 1169/2011, NHCR 1924/2006 health claims) |
| 4 | Provides label templates; basic regulatory guidance |
| 3 | Can review your labels but doesn't create them |
| 2 | No regulatory support; you need your own consultant |
| 1 | Doesn't understand supplement labelling requirements |

---

### 10. Communication Speed (Medium Weight: ×2)

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Responds same day; proactive updates |
| 4 | Responds within 24 hours |
| 3 | Responds within 48 hours |
| 2 | Responds within a week |
| 1 | > 1 week or doesn't respond |

**Why it matters:** Slow communication before you've paid = worse communication after you've paid.

---

### 11. References / Track Record (Low Weight: ×1)

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Provides 3+ references; happy to connect you directly; case studies of similar products |
| 4 | Provides 2 references; willing to connect |
| 3 | Provides 1 reference |
| 2 | Claims references exist but won't share |
| 1 | Refuses to provide any references |

---

### 12. Payment Terms (Low Weight: ×1)

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Net 30 or better; minimal deposit |
| 4 | 50% order, 50% delivery |
| 3 | 50% order, 50% on dispatch |
| 2 | 75% upfront |
| 1 | 100% upfront |
