# Red Flags and Vetting Checklist

> Originally developed for a supplement subscription business, now genericized for universal use.

Use this before every supplier call and after every quote. One red flag doesn't always mean walk away — but two or more should make you pause. Any must-have red flag is an automatic disqualification.

---

## Must-Have Red Flags (Disqualify Immediately)

| # | Red Flag | Why It Matters | What to Do |
|---|----------|---------------|------------|
| 1 | **No verifiable GMP certificate** | Illegal to manufacture food/supplements in the UK without food safety systems. GMP is the baseline. Product recall risk; liability if someone gets sick. | Ask for the certificate number and certifying body. Verify on the certifier's website (SGS, BSI, NSF, BRC). If they can't provide it, walk away. |
| 2 | **100% payment upfront on first order** | You have zero leverage if they fail to deliver, deliver the wrong product, or go bust. | Negotiate 50/50. If they refuse, walk away. |
| 3 | **Refuses to provide a client reference** | Hiding something — bad quality, missed deadlines, or they don't actually have clients. | Always ask for at least one reference. If they refuse, walk away. |
| 4 | **No allergen management plan or documentation** | Cross-contamination risk. Legal liability if a customer with an allergy has a reaction. | Ask for their allergen risk assessment and clean-down validation. If they can't produce it, walk away. |
| 5 | **No physical address or verifiable location** | Potential scam, shell company, or someone operating from a garage. | Google the address. Check Companies House. Ask for a video call from their facility. If they refuse, walk away. |

---

## Serious Red Flags (Proceed with Extreme Caution)

| # | Red Flag | Why It Matters | What to Do |
|---|----------|---------------|------------|
| 6 | **"GMP-equivalent" or "GMP-compliant" but no certificate** | Marketing speak for "we're not actually certified." Common among budget suppliers. | Ask directly: "Can you provide your GMP certificate number?" If they hedge, it's a no. |
| 7 | **Vague or evasive on lead times** | Likely outsources production, is disorganised, or overcommitted. | Push for a specific timeline in writing. If they can't give one, assume 2× whatever they say. |
| 8 | **No Certificate of Analysis (COA) for raw materials** | Can't verify ingredient identity, purity, or potency. You might be selling under-dosed or contaminated product. | Insist on COAs for every batch. If they refuse, walk away. |
| 9 | **Pushes you to order far more than you need** | They're optimising for their revenue, not your success. Classic sign of a supplier who doesn't care about long-term relationships. | Stick to your budget. A good supplier will find a way to make it work. |
| 10 | **Website is broken, outdated, or has no real information** | If they can't maintain a website, how will they maintain your product quality? | Not a dealbreaker on its own, but combined with other flags it's concerning. |
| 11 | **Won't sign an NDA before discussing formulation** | They may share your idea with other clients or use it themselves. | Always send an NDA before sharing proprietary formulations. If they refuse, walk away. |
| 12 | **Extremely low pricing vs. competitors** | You get what you pay for. Cut-rate pricing often means cut-rate ingredients, poor quality control, or hidden fees later. | Get 3+ quotes. If one is 50%+ cheaper, dig into why. Ask about ingredient sources and QC processes. |
| 13 | **No microbiological testing or stability data** | Product could spoil, grow mould, or degrade before the expiry date. | Ask: "Do you conduct microbiological testing on finished product?" "Do you have stability data?" If no, walk away. |
| 14 | **Reluctant to share facility photos or do a video tour** | Hiding poor conditions, outdated equipment, or outsourcing. | Ask for a video call from the production floor. Most legitimate suppliers are proud of their facility. |
| 15 | **No professional indemnity or product liability insurance** | If something goes wrong, you bear all the legal and financial risk. | Ask for proof of insurance. Minimum £1M product liability cover is standard. |

## Yellow Flags (Investigate Further)

| # | Yellow Flag | What to Check |
|---|-------------|---------------|
| 16 | Only accepts bank transfer (no invoice, no contract) | Ask for a formal quote and payment terms in writing. Use a contract. |
| 17 | Communication is slow or unprofessional before you've paid | This will get worse after you've paid. Response speed is a leading indicator. |
| 18 | No experience with powder sachets specifically | They may have great tablet/capsule capabilities but powder is a different discipline. Ask for powder-specific references. |
| 19 | Requires you to buy all packaging materials yourself | Not necessarily bad, but adds complexity. Check if they can source packaging at better rates. |
| 20 | Can't split an order across multiple SKUs | May force you to over-order one SKU to meet MOQ. Ask if they'll do mixed SKUs on one production run. |
| 21 | No experience with subscription or recurring orders | Important for a subscription-based model. Ask if they can handle call-off orders or scheduled production. |
| 22 | Based outside UK/EU | Adds complexity with customs, VAT, and regulatory compliance. Not a dealbreaker but know what you're getting into. |
| 23 | New company (< 2 years old) | Higher risk, but some great new companies exist. Counter-check with references and facility visit. |

## Pre-Call Checklist

Before every supplier call, run through this:

- [ ] Googled the company — any news, reviews, or complaints?
- [ ] Checked Companies House (UK) or equivalent — are they registered?
- [ ] Checked their website — professional, up to date, with real contact details?
- [ ] Checked LinkedIn — do employees look real? How long has the company existed?
- [ ] Prepared my questions (see SKILL.md vetting questions)
- [ ] Sent an NDA if discussing proprietary formulations
- [ ] Set a budget ceiling and stick to it
- [ ] Prepared to ask for a reference

---

## Post-Quote Checklist

After receiving a quote, before responding:

- [ ] All-in cost confirmed — no hidden fees?
- [ ] MOQ is within budget
- [ ] Lead time is acceptable
- [ ] Payment terms are not 100% upfront
- [ ] GMP certificate number provided and verifiable
- [ ] Allergen management plan described
- [ ] Sample availability and cost confirmed
- [ ] At least one reference provided (or promised)
- [ ] Quote is valid for at least 30 days
- [ ] Written quote received (not verbal)
