# Supplier Types: Co-Packer vs. Manufacturer vs. White-Label

> Originally developed for a supplement subscription business, now genericized for universal use.

Choosing the right supplier type for [Your Brand]'s Phase 0 is one of the most important early decisions. Here's the full comparison.

---

## Manufacturer (Custom Formulation)

**What they do:** Source raw materials, develop your formula, blend powders, fill sachets, apply labels — end to end.

**Pros:**
- Full control over formulation and ingredient quality
- Proprietary formula (harder for competitors to copy)
- Can iterate and improve the product over time
- Direct relationship with ingredient suppliers
- Better margins at scale (you own the formula)

**Cons:**
- Highest MOQs (typically 1,000–5,000 units)
- Longest lead times (8–16 weeks including NPD)
- Higher upfront costs (NPD fees, setup fees, minimum raw material orders)
- You bear full regulatory responsibility
- More complexity — you're managing the full supply chain

**Best for:** Brands with validated demand, larger budgets (£10K+ first run), or unique formulations that don't exist off-the-shelf.

**Not for:** [Your Brand] Phase 0 with a limited first-run budget.

---

## Co-Packer (You Source, They Blend + Pack)

**What they do:** You buy the raw materials (or they source on your behalf), they blend, fill sachets, and package.

**Pros:**
- Lower MOQs than full manufacturers (100–1,000 units)
- You control ingredient sourcing and quality
- Faster than custom formulation (4–8 weeks)
- Can start with a simpler formula and iterate
- More transparent cost structure

**Cons:**
- You need to source or specify raw materials
- Still requires some NPD and setup
- MOQs may still be too high for a tight first-run budget
- You're managing more of the supply chain
- Less hand-holding on regulatory

**Best for:** Brands with some experience, a specific ingredient vision, or an existing supplier relationship for raw materials.

**Maybe for:** [Your Brand] if you can find a co-packer willing to do 250 units and you have a simple formula ready.

---

## White-Label (Their Product, Your Brand)

**What they do:** Sell you an existing, finished product with your branding and packaging.

**Pros:**
- Lowest MOQs (50–500 units)
- Fastest to market (2–6 weeks)
- Lowest upfront cost
- No NPD or formulation risk
- Regulatory burden is shared (they hold the spec)
- Can test demand before investing in custom formulation

**Cons:**
- No proprietary formula (competitors can buy the same base product)
- Less control over ingredients and quality
- Ingredient list is fixed — limited customisation
- Margins may be lower per unit
- Harder to differentiate on product alone

**Best for:** Phase 0 launch, demand validation, and brands that differentiate on service/personalisation rather than unique formulations.

**Ideal for:** [Your Brand] Phase 0. Launch with white-label, validate the quiz + subscription model, then migrate to custom formulation once you have revenue and customer feedback.

---

## Hybrid Approach (Recommended for [Your Brand])

**Phase 0 (Months 1–3): White-label**
- Launch with 2–3 white-label SKUs
- Validate demand, pricing, and subscription mechanics
- Learn what customers actually want
- Budget: [£1,500–£2,000]

**Phase 1 (Months 4–6): Co-packer**
- Introduce 1–2 custom or modified formulas via co-packer
- Begin building your ingredient sourcing relationships
- Budget: [£3,000–£5,000] per SKU

**Phase 2 (Months 7–12): Full Manufacturer**
- Migrate core SKUs to custom formulation
- Lock in proprietary blends for differentiation
- Scale to 1,000+ units/month
- Budget: [£10,000+] per production run

## Decision Matrix

| Factor | Manufacturer | Co-Packer | White-Label |
|--------|------------|-----------|-------------|
| **First-run budget needed** | £5,000–£20,000 | £2,000–£10,000 | £500–£3,000 |
| **Time to first sale** | 3–6 months | 2–4 months | 2–6 weeks |
| **MOQ (units)** | 1,000–5,000 | 100–1,000 | 50–500 |
| **Formula control** | Full | Partial | None |
| **Regulatory burden** | High | Medium | Low |
| **Risk level** | High | Medium | Low |
| **Best for Phase 0?** | No | Maybe | **Yes** |

## How to Find Each Type

| Type | Where to Look |
|------|---------------|
| **Manufacturer** | Vitafoods exhibitor list, CPhI, NutraIngredients supplier directory, Google "powder supplement manufacturer UK" |
| **Co-Packer** | UK Contract Packing Association (contractpacking.org.uk), Food Matters Live exhibitors, Google "powder co-packer UK" |
| **White-Label** | Search "white label supplement UK", Alibaba white-label suppliers (vet carefully), ask manufacturers if they offer white-label options |

**Pro tip:** Many full manufacturers also offer white-label products as a side business. Always ask: "Do you have any existing powder formulations we could white-label?" Even if they don't advertise it, they might have off-the-shelf products they'll sell you.
